# Deck archetypes

Select the closest archetype, then tailor the storyline to the decision. Do not treat the page list as a template that must be filled mechanically. Use `references/content-density.md` to set the analytical depth and appendix plan.

Unless the user explicitly requests a brief, use research-heavy consulting mode: each core analytical page should normally contain 4–8 registered evidence items, a meaningful comparison and linked appendix backup where needed.

## Strategy consulting

1. **Executive summary** — 3–5 evidence-backed conclusions, quantified impact, decision required and primary risk.
2. **Context and market change** — historical trend, market size/growth, structural drivers, segment or geography split and alternative forecast.
3. **Core problem / opportunity** — quantify the gap, identify affected value pools and show why the issue matters now.
4. **Internal diagnosis** — decompose performance by product, customer, channel, process or geography; compare with history and peers.
5. **Root causes** — driver tree, variance bridge or process analysis separating symptoms from causal factors.
6. **Strategic options** — feasible choices, economics, capabilities required, risks and switching costs.
7. **Recommended strategy** — choice, rationale, rejected alternatives, conditions for success and no-regret moves.
8. **Financial / operating impact** — base, upside and downside cases with assumptions and sensitivity.
9. **Implementation roadmap** — workstreams, owners, milestones, decision gates, resource needs and critical path.
10. **Risks and mitigations** — probability, impact, leading indicators, owners and residual risk.
11. **Decisions required** — approvals, sequencing choices, investment envelope and immediate next actions.
12. **Appendix** — market definitions, detailed analyses, source tables, scenario assumptions and rejected options.

Minimum evidence pack: market history, customer or segment economics, peer benchmark, internal performance baseline, option economics, implementation requirements and risk evidence.

## IT / cloud transformation

1. **Executive summary** — quantified current-state issue, target outcome, recommended transformation path and decision required.
2. **Business and technology context** — demand growth, strategic priorities, service constraints, regulatory or security requirements.
3. **Current-state baseline** — application portfolio, infrastructure, data estate, cost, reliability, security and delivery metrics.
4. **Pain-point decomposition** — incidents, lead time, unit cost, technical debt, capacity, skills and vendor dependencies.
5. **Workload segmentation** — classify applications and data by value, complexity, risk, lifecycle and migration disposition.
6. **Target outcomes and KPIs** — measurable target ranges for cost, speed, resilience, security, productivity and sustainability.
7. **Target architecture** — design choices, interfaces, control points, standards, trade-offs and quantified service targets.
8. **Migration / modernization waves** — workload counts, sequencing logic, exit criteria, dependencies and factory capacity.
9. **Platform and operating model** — roles, product teams, governance, FinOps, SecOps, SRE and adoption mechanisms.
10. **TCO / business case** — baseline spend, one-off cost, recurring cost, benefits, scenarios, payback and sensitivity.
11. **Vendor / sourcing implications** — capability fit, commercial model, lock-in, portability and negotiation levers.
12. **Risks and controls** — migration, security, data, continuity, skills, adoption and benefits-realization risks.
13. **Roadmap and decisions** — phases, budget envelope, owners, gates and immediate actions.
14. **Appendix** — application inventory summary, cost model, architecture standards, wave logic, RACI and assumptions.

Minimum evidence pack: current-state inventory, cost baseline, service-performance baseline, workload segmentation, vendor capability evidence, pricing/TCO inputs, target KPI rationale and delivery-capacity assumptions.

## AI transformation

1. **Executive summary** — value thesis, prioritized domains, platform/governance recommendation and quantified expected impact.
2. **Business-value baseline** — process volumes, labor effort, error rates, cycle time, revenue leakage or service gaps.
3. **Use-case landscape** — comprehensive inventory by function, user, value mechanism, risk and data readiness.
4. **Use-case prioritization** — weighted scoring with evidence behind each score and sensitivity to weights.
5. **Wave 1 use cases** — detailed value model, user workflow, data requirements, controls and adoption assumptions.
6. **Data and knowledge readiness** — source quality, entitlement, freshness, retrieval performance and remediation effort.
7. **Model and platform choices** — model fit, cost, latency, quality, deployment pattern, portability and evaluation evidence.
8. **Target architecture** — orchestration, data/knowledge, models, observability, security, policy and integration decisions.
9. **Responsible AI and controls** — risk taxonomy, evaluation gates, human oversight, auditability and incident response.
10. **Operating model** — centralized assets, domain product teams, ownership, funding and reuse mechanisms.
11. **Economics** — inference cost, platform cost, implementation effort, adoption curve, productivity capture and scenarios.
12. **Roadmap** — pilots, scale gates, platform releases, change plan, KPI targets and dependencies.
13. **Risks and decisions** — failure modes, leading indicators, mitigation owners and approvals required.
14. **Appendix** — use-case scorecards, prompt/evaluation methods, model benchmarks, cost assumptions and architecture detail.

Minimum evidence pack: process baselines, use-case value assumptions, model or platform benchmarks, pricing, security/regulatory requirements, customer or implementation evidence, adoption assumptions and counter-evidence on productivity capture.

## Sales proposal / pre-sales deck

1. **Client situation and quantified challenges** — client-specific or industry-supported baseline, not generic pain points.
2. **Business value hypothesis** — value pools, measurable outcomes and assumptions requiring validation.
3. **Why change now** — strategic, regulatory, competitive, cost or technology triggers with evidence.
4. **Proposed solution** — solution components mapped explicitly to client requirements and pain points.
5. **Architecture and delivery model** — integration, security, data flows, operating responsibilities and service targets.
6. **Use cases / scope** — prioritized scenarios, users, volumes, requirements and acceptance criteria.
7. **Differentiators** — evidence-backed comparison versus status quo and alternatives; avoid unsupported marketing claims.
8. **Implementation roadmap** — deliverables, owners, dependencies, client commitments, gates and timeline.
9. **Investment and ROI** — commercial assumptions, implementation effort, operating cost, benefits and scenarios.
10. **Risk management** — delivery, adoption, security, data, commercial and dependency risks.
11. **Proof and references** — relevant customer cases, benchmarks, product documentation and limitations.
12. **Commercial next steps** — decision path, discovery needs, proposal assumptions and immediate actions.
13. **Appendix** — detailed requirements mapping, architecture, service descriptions, pricing assumptions and case references.

Minimum evidence pack: client or industry baseline, product documentation, price and consumption assumptions, implementation estimate, quantified value model, competitive comparison and relevant customer evidence.

## Investment / equity analysis

1. **Investment summary** — thesis, valuation, expected return, catalysts, risks and invalidation conditions.
2. **Company overview** — business model, segment economics, geographic mix and key dependencies.
3. **Industry context** — market growth, competitive structure, regulation, supply/demand and industry-cycle position.
4. **Revenue and operating drivers** — volume, price, mix, customer, capacity and unit economics.
5. **Financial performance** — 3–5 years or all available periods for revenue, margin, cash flow, capex, leverage and returns.
6. **Segment and peer benchmark** — compare growth, margin, capital intensity, valuation and balance-sheet quality.
7. **Management guidance and execution** — prior guidance versus actuals, strategic commitments and credibility indicators.
8. **Capital allocation** — dividend, buyback, debt, M&A, reinvestment and shareholder-return priorities.
9. **Forecast scenarios** — base, bull and bear operating forecasts with explicit assumptions.
10. **Valuation** — DCF and/or multiples, historical range, peer comparison and sensitivity.
11. **Catalysts and monitoring indicators** — dated events, operating milestones and evidence that would change the thesis.
12. **Risks and downside** — scenario impact, probability, mitigation and thesis invalidation.
13. **Investment conclusion** — position sizing logic, time horizon, expected total return and conditions for action.
14. **Appendix** — financial statements, segment model, peer table, valuation detail, source definitions and tax considerations.

Minimum evidence pack: filings, earnings materials, management commentary, historical financials, consensus or clearly labeled independent estimates, peer data, valuation inputs, capital-return history and credible counter-thesis evidence.

## Vendor / option evaluation

1. **Decision context** — scope, constraints, non-negotiables and expected outcome.
2. **Requirements and evaluation criteria** — weighted criteria with rationale and evidence needs.
3. **Options considered** — viable alternatives, deployment models and status quo.
4. **Capability comparison** — requirement-by-requirement evidence from documentation, demos, references or tests.
5. **Commercial / TCO comparison** — licenses, consumption, services, migration, operations and switching costs.
6. **Architecture and integration fit** — interfaces, identity, data, security, operations and portability.
7. **Risk / dependency analysis** — maturity, roadmap dependency, lock-in, compliance, skills and vendor viability.
8. **Scoring and sensitivity** — weighted results, evidence behind scores and conditions that change the recommendation.
9. **Recommended option** — rationale, rejected alternatives, negotiation points and safeguards.
10. **Implementation plan** — pilot, migration, acceptance tests, exit criteria and governance.
11. **Appendix** — detailed requirement matrix, test results, source extracts, pricing assumptions and contract questions.

Minimum evidence pack: official capability documentation, price model, technical validation, security/compliance evidence, customer references, operating requirements, contract dependencies and credible limitations.

## Executive decision meeting

1. **Decision required** — precise approval or choice, deadline and consequence of delay.
2. **Situation and complication** — quantified facts and why the current path is insufficient.
3. **Options** — economics, feasibility, timing, risks and reversibility.
4. **Recommendation** — chosen option, rationale and rejected alternative.
5. **Financial / operating impact** — scenarios, assumptions and sensitivity.
6. **Implementation implications** — owners, resources, dependencies and near-term milestones.
7. **Risks and mitigations** — leading indicators, owners and residual risk.
8. **Required approval** — decision wording, funding, governance and next actions.
9. **Appendix** — detailed analysis and source backup.

## Workshop material

1. **Objectives and decisions to unlock** — define the workshop output, not only agenda.
2. **Evidence-based context primer** — key facts, trends, constraints and unresolved questions.
3. **Diagnostic views** — pre-read analysis that helps participants start from a common fact base.
4. **Discussion questions** — questions tied to decisions and evidence gaps.
5. **Breakout structure** — teams, inputs, method, timebox and expected outputs.
6. **Templates / exercises** — structured scoring, prioritization, process mapping or scenario tools.
7. **Synthesis page** — decision criteria, areas of agreement, open issues and evidence required.
8. **Follow-up actions** — owners, deadlines, deliverables and escalation path.
9. **Appendix** — source pack, definitions, detailed data and facilitator notes.

Workshop pages may be less data-dense than analytical decks, but the context primer and diagnostic pages must still meet the evidence standard.
